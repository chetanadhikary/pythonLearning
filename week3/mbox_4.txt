The organizing committee for the upcoming Open Source Geospatial '05 
conference would like to open nominations for the inaugural Sol Katz 
Award for Geospatial Free and Open Source Software.

The Sol Katz Award for Geospatial Free and Open Source Software (GFOSS) 
will be given to individuals who have demonstrated leadership in the 
GFOSS community. Recipients of the award will have contributed 
significantly through their activities to advance open source ideals in 
the geospatial realm.

Sol Katz was an early pioneer of GFOSS and left behind a large body of 
work in the form of applications, format specifications, and utilities 
while at the U.S. Bureau of Land Management.  This early GFOSS archive 
provided both source code and applications freely available to the 
community.  Sol was also a frequent contributor to many geospatial list 
servers, providing much guidance to the geospatial community at large.

Sol unfortunately passed away in 1999 from Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma, but 
his legacy lives on in the open source world. Those interested in making 
a donation to the American Cancer Society, as per Sol's family's 
request, can do so at
https://www.cancer.org/asp/donate/don_multi_donate.asp?navToScreen=don_1.

Nominations for the Sol Katz Award should be sent to 
sol.katz.award@gmail.com with a brief description of the reasons for 
this nomination.  Please place "Sol Katz Award" inthe subject of the 
email.  Nominations will be accepted until Saturday April 30th 
@midnight.  A recipient will be decided from the nomination list by a 
selection committee.

The winner of the Sol Katz Award for Geospatial Free and Open Source 
Software will be announced at the Open Source Geospatial conference in 
June.  The hope is that the award will both acknowledge the work of 
community members, and pay tribute to one of its founders, for years to 
come.

Hello MapServer users in Ottawa!

It has been a very long time since our last meeting (blush), so we're 
definitely way overdue for one.  Many things have happened in the Open 
Source GIS world recently, so we're bound to have lots to talk about and 
catch up on.

The meeting is planned for Thurs July 20th at 6pm, here at the office of 
DM Solutions (http://www.dmsolutions.ca/about/contact.html).  Please 
email me directly if you plan to attend.

The Ottawa Grass Users Group (OGUG) will also be attending this meeting 
- for those of you not familiar with the group, they are a very lively 
bunch that have already gained recognition with their work on the Public 
Geodata License.  I personally think it would be great to join forces 
with them, for many reasons.  (for more info on OGUG: 
http://cemml.carleton.ca:8080/OGUG)

On that topic, in this meeting we will all be discussing the possibility 
of the creation of a local Open Source Geospatial Foundation (OSGeo) 
chapter, to promote Open Source geospatial use in the Ottawa region.  It 
should be a great discussion.  (for more info on OSGeo: 
http://www.osgeo.org/)

Here is the proposed itinerary with proposed presenters next to each
item. Please let us know if you would like anything added.

1. Welcome and group intros (Dave S and Jeff)
2. What is OSGEO and what does it mean to be a local chapter (Frank W)
3. Backgrounder: OGUG (David M)
4. Backgrounder: OMSUG (Volunteer?)
5. Why merge the two (Jeff M)
6. Next steps in making an official chapter (Jeff, Dave, Frank)
7. Orders of business for next meeting. (Dave)
8. Determination of next meeting (August?)
9. Beer (all)

I think it will be a fun meeting, with some life and purpose (not that 
all of those meetings with beer and wings didn't serve any purpose haha).
